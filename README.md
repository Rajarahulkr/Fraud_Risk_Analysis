ğŸ“ˆ Key Insights (Data Analyst Perspective)

Most customers fall into the Standard credit category, while Good and Poor scorers show sharply different financial behavior.

Outstanding Debt, EMI, and Utilization Ratio clearly separate Good and Poor credit scorers.

Income alone is not a strong predictor of credit score; repayment discipline matters more.

Customers paying only minimum dues are significantly more likely to have poor credit scores.

Frequent payment delays are strong indicators of financial stress and default risk.

Customers with multiple loan types and heavy reliance on credit cards/personal loans tend to belong to weaker credit segments.

Correlation analysis confirmed debt and utilization metrics as critical risk indicators.
